# Pathways & Special Cases

## SDEC: Blood transfusions
- Book onto **medical tracker** for the SDEC team to contact.
- **Do not give an arrival time.**
- Ensure ED bloods include **2 × Group & Save** (helps quicker requesting of red cells).

## PE / DVT in pregnancy
- **≤16 weeks gestation:** route to **SDEC**
- **>16 weeks gestation** and **≤6 weeks postpartum:** via **Obstetrics registrar**
- Ensure the patient information leaflet is given (as referenced in the guide).

## Frailty
- **In hours:** refer to **Frailty (bleep 544)** if medical input required; or **PT/OT (bleep 290)** if therapies only.
- **Out of hours:** if patient requires input from these services, refer via **Medical Registrar** so patient comfort/rest and clerking/prescribing is addressed.
- If admitted to **DAU**, the treating clinician is responsible for prescribing regular meds; ensure time-critical meds are prescribed promptly.

## Infectious Diseases: Suspected TB
- If suspected TB (clinical presentation and/or CXR findings), email referral to **uhdb.tbserviceburton@nhs.net**
- Weekdays: TB specialist nurse extension **5050**
- If admission required: use acute medical intake as usual.
- Reporting radiographers may also refer to the TB service via email if concerned.

## Infectious Diseases: Rabies vaccine pathway (Rabipur)
- Rabies vaccines (**RABIPUR**) are kept in the POH cupboard fridge.
- Administer dose **IM**.
- Complete the risk assessment; contact **RIgS (UKHSA)** on **0330 128 1020**.
  - RIgS will take patient details, GP address, and an NHS email to send the completed assessment (attach to records).
  - They will send 2 vials to the patient’s GP and advise dose dates, and replace the used Rabipur.
- **Weekends/Bank Holidays 9am–7pm:** contact **Colindale Duty Doctor Service** via **0208 200 4400** instead of RIgS.
- Note in guide: UK is not an endemic rabies nation; immunoglobulins not usually given and not held in hospital.

## Dermatology: photography & IG compliance (out of hours)
- In hours: use V6 to request medical photography.
- Out of hours options:
  1) Place V6 order; patient contacts medical photography next day via switchboard extension **5299**
  2) Take photos via Outlook (IG compliant) and email **uhdb.qhbphotographic@nhs.net** with name, NHS number, DOB, urgency, and date taken.
- Can also be used for safeguarding concerns OOH or transfer needs (e.g. burns).
