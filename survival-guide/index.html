    Frailty:

    In hours patients can be referred frailty (544) if medical input required, or if therapies only then to PT / OT (290). Out of hours patient requiring input from these services must be referred to the medical registrar to ensure comfort and rest as well as being clerked by the medical team so regular medications are prescribed.

    If patients are being admitted to DAU then it is the treating clinicians responsibility to prescribe all the regular medications. Please all ensure time critical medications are prescribed promptly.

    Infectious Diseases:

    QHB Internal suspected TB referral pathway:

    Patients suspecting to have TB (based on clinical presentation and/or CXR findings, please send an email referral to uhdb.tbserviceburton@nhs.net.

    During weekdays, you can contact TB specialist nurse on extension 5050.

    If these patients need hospital admission, must go through acute medical intake as usual. (chest physicians)

    If the reporting radiographers are worried about CXR findings suspicious of TB, they now will refer to the burton TB service team via email.

    QHB Rabies vaccine pathway:

    Usually, patients present to ED with h/o dog or wild animal bites when on recent holiday and was advised to get Rabies vaccines when returned to UK.

    We keep rabies vaccines (RABIPUR) in our POH cupboard (3-4 vials in the Fridge). We must take one form these and administer the dose as IM injection.

    We then need to complete the risk assessment; the assessing clinician should then contact RIgS at UKHSA via 0330 128 1020

    RIgS will take details of the patient, their GP surgery address and our NHS email address (to which they will send the completed risk assessment from which needs to be attached to the patient records). They will send 2 vials to the patient's GP surgery and advice which dates they need to have these 2 doses. They will also replace the used Rabipur vaccine from QHB POH cupboard.

    Weekends and Bank Holidays 9am – 7pm (Colindale Duty Doctor Service hours) Follow the above process, but instead of contacting RIgS, contact the Colindale Duty Doctor Service via 0208 200 4400.

    Note: UK is not an endemic nation for Rabies and so we do not usually give "Immunoglobulins" and so do not have in hour hospital.

    Major Trauma (Paediatric) 

    Birmingham Children's Hospital is our local Paediatric Major Trauma Centre.  

    ALL children with trauma needing a transfer to the Children’s MTC (Birmingham Children’s Hospital) should be referred via KIDS (Kids Critical Care and Intensive Support). 

    Do not phone ED or a Specialty area or the RTD. 

    KIDS Tel: 0300 200 1100 

    KIDS will facilitate the call and provide TTL advice when required 

    KIDS will be used in a coordination capacity and will not necessarily move the child. If the patient is time critical it will still fall on the local team to transfer
